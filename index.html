Our Mission

\

Our mission is to open the door to STEM for anyone who is curious but doesn't know where to start. We turn big ideas in science, technology, and engineering into simple explanations, real-world examples, and hands-on exploration. We believe that STEM should feel less like a textbook and more like something you can play with, question, and understand. We also teach material that schools may not always cover, especially for students who don't have access to those kinds of opportunities.

BIO

Biology

Life Science
\
\
What is it?
\

Biology is the study of life and living things, from tiny bacteria that can only be seen with a microscope to giant whales and elephants. It helps us understand how living things grow and change, such as a caterpillar turning into a butterfly. Biology also teaches us how our bodies work, like how the heart pumps blood around the body to keep us alive and healthy. It shows how plants, animals, and people are connected to nature, such as when bees help flowers grow by carrying pollen from one flower to another.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

Biology is a very broad topic, and biologists study living things in many different ways. Some work in labs using microscopes to examine cells and bacteria, while others study animals and plants in their natural habitats. They may run experiments, collect samples, or analyze data to understand how life works. Biologists can work in research, healthcare, conservation, or education.

CS

Computer Science

Technology
\
\
What is it?
\

Computer science is the study of computers, technology, and how information is processed. It teaches us how to design programs, solve problems, and create systems that help people in their everyday lives. Computer science explains how apps, websites (like this one!), video games, and AI work.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

People in this field are often called software developers, computer scientists, or programmers. They spend their time writing code, testing programs, and fixing bugs. Some create apps, websites, and video games, while others work in areas like cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and data analysis.

CHEM

Chemistry

Physical Science
\
\
What is it?
\

Chemistry is the study of matter and how different substances interact and change. It helps us understand what everything around us is made of, from the air we breathe to the food we eat. Chemistry explains why certain reactions happen, such as baking a cake or rust forming on metal.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

Chemists study substances and how they change and react. Many work in labs where they mix chemicals, run experiments, and carefully record what happens. Some help develop new medicines, while others work on creating better materials, safer products, or cleaner energy sources.

\
\
\
\
ENG

Engineering

Design & Build
\
\
What is it?
\

Engineering is the application of science, mathematics, and technology to design, build, and improve structures, machines, systems, and processes. It combines creativity, problem-solving, and technical knowledge to develop solutions that meet human needs and solve real-world challenges.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

Engineers design, develop, test, and improve products, systems, and structures to solve real-world problems. Depending on their field, they may work on buildings, machines, transportation systems, computers, energy solutions, or medical devices.

\
\
\
\
ENV

Environmental Science

Earth & Climate
\
\
What is it?
\

Environmental science is the study of the natural world and how humans interact with it. It helps us understand ecosystems, natural resources, weather patterns, and the impact people have on the environment. It explores topics such as pollution, climate change, conservation, and biodiversity.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

Environmental scientists, ecologists, and conservationists study nature, collect samples, and analyze data about air, water, soil, plants, and animals. Many focus on solving problems like pollution, climate change, and habitat loss to help protect the environment.

\
\
\
\
PHY

Physics

Forces & Energy
\
\
What is it?
\

Physics is the study of matter, energy, forces, and motion. It helps us understand how and why things move, from a ball rolling across the ground to planets orbiting the Sun. Physics explains everyday phenomena such as gravity, electricity, and light.

\
What do people in this field do?
\

Physicists study how forces, energy, and motion work in the universe. Many spend their days doing experiments, building models, or using computers to test ideas. They help develop technologies from medical imaging machines to satellites and renewable energy systems.

👩‍💻
Sophie Ma
Founder, STEMExplorer

Hi, my name is Sophie, and I'm the founder of STEMExplorer. Growing up with both of my parents working as computer science professors sparked my curiosity about technology and innovation from a very young age. I was always surrounded by conversations about problem-solving and writing research papers, which inspired me to explore STEM for myself.

Even though I always knew I wanted to pursue a STEM-related field, I often found the subject extremely overwhelming. For me, the idea of science and engineering felt mentally demanding, and I wasn't always sure where to begin. I also realized that I didn't fully understand what each STEM field actually involved or what different career paths looked like. It was difficult to find clear, accessible resources that explained these opportunities in a way that felt approachable.

Why STEMExplorer exists

That's why I created STEMExplorer. I wanted to build the resource I wish I had when I was starting out: a place where students can learn about different STEM fields, discover opportunities to explore more deeply, and foster their ideas without feeling intimidated or discouraged.

Not everyone has access to great science programs at school. Not everyone knows which questions to ask, or where to start. STEMExplorer exists to make STEM more accessible and understandable for everyone, regardless of their background or prior experience. Whether you're interested in engineering, computer science, medicine, environmental science, mathematics, or something else entirely, this platform is designed to help you find your path.
